**Observation on Determination and Deliberate Decision-making:**  
If we consciously and deliberately make decisions, carefully taking one step at a time, it follows through simple induction that the initial step is often the most challenging. Yet, with a robust foundation established, each subsequent step becomes progressively easier, eventually liberating us from constraints we initially faced.  

Such deliberate, thoughtful action ensures that each decision informs and enriches subsequent choices, broadening our options and enhancing adaptability in diverse scenarios. After all, increasing our available options can hardly be detrimental.  

Moreover, deliberate decision-making introduces additional vectors of control, notably granting us the freedom to decide *when* we choose, thus enabling us to pace and structure our steps effectively.
